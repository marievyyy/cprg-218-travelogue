01 Paris France

Our Paris trip was absolutely amazing! I soaked up all the culture and history. Each day of this trip brought something new for me to discover.

Day 1 Ofcourse we had to start our trip with visiting a café. We head out to get some buttery croissants from Pain Pain and cappuccinos from a quaint little café. After that we walked to Sacre-Coeur and through the Montmartre neighborhood (fun fact: it's where the famous John Wick 4 stairs scene was filmed). Then we had unforgettable lunch at Chez Janou (highly recommended this).   Our afternoon was We started to check things off our list, so our afternoon activity was packed! We started by exploring the Louvre Gardens followed by a tour of the Louvre Museum at 3:30 PM (hello, Mona Lisa!). Afterward, we climbed to the top of the Arc de Triomphe and walked back to the Eiffel Tower area in the 7th Arrondissement. I was a very surreal moment. Steps count: 38,560 steps (I didn't plan on resting for this vacation hehe)

Day 2We dedicated the entire day exploring the grandeur of Versailles, and it is such a memorable experience. The lavish palace and the meticulously maintained gardens were absolutely breathtaking. We had a quick lunch at the on-site café to give us a chance to recharge before continuing our adventure. It was a long day, but absolutely worth it! I definitely would recommend visiting if you plan to visit Paris.

Day 3On our third day, we decided to take it easy. We started our day with a delicious almond croissant and a cup of coffee at Café Richard. After that, we visited the stunning stained-glass windows of Sainte-Chapelle and explored the charming streets of the Latin Quarter. For lunch, we treated ourselves to some ramen at Kodawari Ramen. In the afternoon we visited the Musée d'Orsay and finally got to see some famous art of Claude Monet and Vincent Van Gogh. Then we head out to Palace des Invalides and we wrapped up the day with a lovely dinner at La Potager.

02 Rome Italy

Rome had a magical way of pulling us in with its rich history culture and mouthwatering food (it is Eataly for a reason!). We stroll through Vatican City to wandering through the lively streets of Trastevere. This was a trip full of moments I will always remember.

Day 1 Our first day in Rome started early to beat the crowds at St. Peter's Basilica. The church was absolutely breathtaking with its stunning architecture and intricate details. It was the most incredible church I've ever seen! From there, we explored the Sistine Chapel and Vatican Museums with a guided tour to fully absorb its history and art. In the afternoon, we climbed to the top of Castel Sant'Angelo for breathtaking views of the Vatican and Rome. Then we had lunch at Pastacuitta, and dinner was a must-try at Pizza de Simone (pasta and pizza duh).

Day 2 On the second day, we visited the Colosseum and were blown away by its ancient beauty. After that, we wandered through the historic Roman Forum, and it feels like we were stepping back in time. In the afternoon, we took a fun break to Trastevere, where we had a dinner at Tonarello (We are lucky to be early because this one had a long line and there' a reason for it! So plan accordingly if you want to eat here). And of course, no trip to Italy is complete without some gelato, so we have to try Italy's gelalo. Steps count: 37, 982 steps

Day 3Day 3 was a whirlwind of exploring Rome's must-see spots. Let's continue checking off our list! We kicked things off by tossing a coin at the Trevi Fountain it is a tradition we couldn't miss. We arrived there 6AM and it is already packed with tourists. After that, we wandered around the Piazza Navona. From there, we took a stroll through Campo de' Fiori to soak in the lively market atmosphere and check out the nearby churches. For lunch, we had a lovely meal at Alfredo e Ada, and to end the day, we treated ourselves to a delicious dinner at Bottigliera.

Day 4Day 4 was a total blast! We left Rome and headed to Tivoli for a chill day trip. We started our day with a coffee at a cozy local café. Then, we had lunch at Osteria Mom, which was the perfect way to get ready for Tivoli. The peaceful surroundings were a welcome change of pace, and we felt like we were in a whole different side of the world. We ended the day back in Rome with a delicious dinner at L'Aquila d'Oro, and it was the perfect way to wrap up our serene and escape from the city.

Day 5Day 5, it was our last full day in Rome, so we had to make it memorable. We kicked things off with a delicious maritozzo (which finally crossed off everything on my food list) and a cup of coffee. We visited the Borghese Museum and immersed ourselves in its stunning collection of art and sculpture. After conquering the famous Spanish Steps, we ate at Pastacuitta before checking out the Palazzo Colonna. For our final dinner, we returned to Va Do to savour one last incredible meal and reflect on our unforgettable trip.Pasta count for the whole trip: 8 pasta
